CORE PHILOSOPHY
NARRATIVE OVER PATTERN-TRADING

This is NOT pattern trading. The MODEL is the consistent framework, but every trade is DIFFERENT — what you actually trade is the NARRATIVE: the real-time story of why price should deliver to a specific draw on liquidity, built from the day's confluences (HTF context, manipulation, time anchor, which liquidity is the draw, imbalances/key levels) plus experience and intuition. Same model, different narrative each session. If you can't articulate the narrative — why THIS delivery, to THIS target, NOW — you don't have a trade, you have a pattern guess. The checklist and model stack probability; the narrative is what you're trading.

THE MARKET IS ENGINEERED

The market is PREDETERMINED / algorithmic, not random. If markets were truly driven by buyers vs sellers, profitable traders would be 50/50 and every day random — you could never have a repeatable model. Because there IS a repeatable time-and-price model, price delivery is engineered, not random.

EDGE = STACKING PROBABILITY. You can trade inside OR outside a model or kill zone. The point of the model + kill zone + checklist is to stack confluences in your favor, lifting win rate from a random ~30-50% up to ~60-70%+. More factors stacked = higher probability. Never force a trade just because the clock says kill zone; never refuse one purely because it isn't — but know your odds.

A kill zone = the last 10 min AND first 10 min of every 1-hour candle (xx:50–xx:09). Highest-probability time for a move — NOT a mandatory gate. The stack: HTF draw on liquidity → daily profile (AMD) → manipulation → time anchor → entry trigger, with kill-zone / macro timing layered on top. Each layer raises probability; no single layer is a signal by itself.

THE MODELS

Discretion, not patterns: these models are a frame of mind, not rigid patterns. Don't mechanically pattern-trade them — it's not always 1 + 1 = 2. Read the NARRATIVE and apply discretion (see Core Philosophy). The model is the frame; the narrative is what you trade.

There are exactly FIVE models: 9:30 Manipulation (read-only) · 9:30 Expansion · 10:00 Reversal · 10:00 Continuation · 10:00 Double Purge (the 5th and last). The Market-Maker Buy/Sell profiles at the bottom are STRUCTURE, not models. Every model applies to EACH 4H candle — 02 · 06 · 10 · 14 · 18 · 22, not just the morning — and that candle's opening price can be used as an order block for the model running on it.

9:30 MANIPULATION READ ONLY — NOT TRADED

NOT a trade you take. Its job is to READ whether the market is being manipulated (is there a Judas swing at 9:30?). Use it to confirm manipulation and get your narrative — do NOT enter on it.

9:30 EXPANSION TRADE — CONDITIONAL

Tradeable ONLY if price RETRACES into the first presented fair value gap (the 9:30 gap). If there is no retrace into that gap → NO trade; wait for the 9:50 macro or 10:00. Two entry options: (a) off the first presented FVG, OR (b) use the 9:30 opening price as an order block and take the trade off that 9:30 opening price.

10:00 REVERSAL MAIN — TRADE

The MAIN model you trade. Can begin from 9:50 (the macro) because the reversal can start before the 4H candle opens at 10:00. HOLD for longer; targets EXTERNAL liquidity (session / day highs and lows). The 4H candle gives room to run to external targets. Entry: off 10:00 + the first presented FVG, OR use the 10:00 opening price as an order block. Direction gate — LONG (up): price must be HOLDING ABOVE the 10:00 opening price AND above the first presented FVG. SHORT (down) is the mirror: price holding BELOW both. Opening price as an order block: close ABOVE the 10:00 opening price while bullish → use it as an order block to keep going higher; close BELOW it while bearish → use it as an order block to keep going lower. If price then can't hold that direction / loses momentum, that same level flips and acts as a BREAKER block. Note: sometimes price will NOT retest the first presented gap at all — in that case you simply use the 10:00 opening price as the order block.

10:00 CONTINUATION TRADE

Sibling of the 10:00 Reversal — same 9:50 macro / 10:00 4H-open core, trading continuation of the higher-timeframe draw when price continues rather than reverses. Rule of thumb: INTERNAL liquidity (gaps, opening prices) → quick scalp; EXTERNAL liquidity (highs/lows) → hold longer.

10:00 DOUBLE PURGE TRADE — REVERSAL

The failure-of-continuation reversal — the 5th and LAST model. Setup: the target is NOT yet hit, but price can't keep pushing higher OR lower — it stalls at the 9:30 / 10:00 window. The tell: price fails to hold above the first presented gap + the 10:00 opening-price order block (for an expected long) — the continuation is failing. The trade: instead of continuing the same direction (don't buy the top), take the OPPOSITE direction — the failed continuation becomes the reversal. Example: 9:30 Expansion → 10:00 Continuation expected, but at 10:00 price can't reach targets and can't hold above the first FVG + 10:00 opening-price OB → Double Purge → take the short instead of buying the top.

BIAS — DIRECTIONAL FRAMEWORK

Bias is NOT one direction you must marry. It is layered:

  • HTF compass — Weekly / Monthly: the long-term directional compass. It gives context: the draw on liquidity and which side is being delivered over days/weeks.
  • Daily / 4H — the delivery you actually trade: you execute the candle in front of you. Even on a day that is bullish on the Daily, you can take a SHORT on the 4H if that 4H candle is delivering down.

RULE: respect the HTF compass for context, but trade the candle in front of you — a valid lower-timeframe setup is not cancelled by a higher-timeframe bias.

TIME ANCHORS & 4H OPENS
  • 09:00 — 9:00 AM opening price: an IMPORTANT key level.
  • 09:30 — RTH open. Home of the 9:30 Manipulation and 9:30 Expansion models.
  • 09:50 — 9:50 macro. The 10:00 reversal can begin trading FROM 9:50 (reversal can start before the 4H candle opens).
  • 10:00 — institutional 4H candle open. Core of the 10:00 Reversal / Continuation model.
  • 10:50–11:10, 11:50–12:10 (Tier-2 backup) and 12:50–13:10 — mid-morning / afternoon macro delivery blocks.
  • 18:00 and 00:00 (Midnight) — opening-price anchors.

ALL FOUR-HOUR CANDLES ARE TRADEABLE: every new 4H institutional candle open is a valid time-and-price anchor, not just the morning. NY 4H opens: 02:00 · 06:00 · 10:00 · 14:00 · 18:00 · 22:00.

HIGHER-TIMEFRAME SPONSORSHIP

Every delivery needs higher-timeframe sponsorship — an HTF Premium/Discount array (a PDRA: a 4H FVG, order block, etc.) that the move draws from or to. Premium vs discount is contextual — read it against the RANGE and the HTF PD array, not statically.

Key insight: an HTF array's role depends on the directional narrative, not just where price sits in a range. Example: a 4H bullish FVG — even if price is in deep discount within a range, if the bias is bearish and price taps up into that higher-timeframe gap, the gap can act as an area of discount that sponsors a further move DOWN. And vice-versa: a bearish HTF array in premium can sponsor a further move UP on a bullish narrative.

Takeaway: don't read premium/discount off the range alone — the HTF PD array is the sponsor; the narrative decides which way it fuels.

A+ 5-PART CHECKLIST

FIRST — THE GATE: Can I articulate the NARRATIVE? (why this delivery, to this target, now — confluences + read, not shape-matching). If you can't articulate it, you don't have a trade — you have a pattern guess.

  1. Isolate the higher-timeframe Draw on Liquidity (Daily/Weekly/4H). The DOL is NOT only session/day highs & lows — targets also include the 9:30 high or low, the 9:00 AM pre-market high/low, INTERNAL liquidity (the 9:30 opening price, the 9:30 first presented gap / FVG, the 10:00 opening price), and imbalances (fair value gaps), opening prices and key levels generally. Rule: internal draws → quick scalp; external draws (highs/lows) → 10:00 hold.
  2. Identify the active daily profile (AMD — Accumulation/Manipulation/Distribution); confirm the Asian session consolidated to build the liquidity baseline.
  3. Verify institutional manipulation: a fast counter-trend Judas leg that sweeps the Asian range / a short-term high or low, or taps an HTF fair value gap (read this via the 9:30 manipulation model).
  4. Enforce the time-and-price anchor: manipulation/entry coincides with a fixed anchor (9:00, 9:30, 9:50, 10:00, a 4H open, 6PM, or Midnight).
  5. Confirm the entry trigger on the execution timeframe (1m/15s): inversion FVG or opening-price rejection. Kill-zone / macro timing adds probability (not required).

All five stack the probability.

TIME-BASED EXITS & RUNNERS

Time-based exit discipline: exit when the delivery block ends or the target is hit — regardless of how much momentum seems left.

LEAVE A RUNNER: you do NOT have to fully close at the time-based exit. Scale out at the target / block-end and leave a runner — let a portion run in case delivery extends. Banking most of the position is mandatory; closing every last contract is not.

PROHIBITED CONDITIONS
  • FOMC weeks and immediate high-impact news = banned. True no-trade — volatile graveyards.
  • First week of the calendar month = LOWER PROBABILITY — NOT a no-trade window. Don't expect big expansion; take normal SCALPS instead of swinging for external targets.
  • After the primary intraday objective is hit = stop; no late-session consolidation trades (the late-session graveyard).
